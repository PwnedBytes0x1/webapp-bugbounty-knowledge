# Triage Interaction

Professional triage interaction is crucial for getting bounties accepted and building your reputation.

## The Triage Process

1. **Report submitted** — Program receives your report
2. **Initial triage** (hours-days) — Validation team reviews
3. **Technical review** (days-weeks) — Engineering team analyzes
4. **Bounty decision** — Amount determined based on severity
5. **Fix deployed** — Development team patches
6. **Disclosure** — Report may be made public (with permission)

## Communicating with Triage

### Do's
- **Be respectful** — They're doing their job
- **Be responsive** — Answer questions within 24 hours
- **Provide additional info when asked** — New requests, curl commands
- **Be patient** — Some programs take weeks to triage

### Don'ts
- Don't demand higher bounties
- Don't threaten public disclosure
- Don't argue about severity (present facts, not demands)
- Don't submit the same bug multiple times

## Handling Rejection

Reports can be rejected for many reasons:

| Reason | What to Do |
|--------|------------|
| Duplicate | Accept it; move on. Could be a good writeup for your blog |
| Informative | Ask for clarification; intent vs impact matters |
| Not applicable | It depends on the context; try a different angle |
| Out of scope | Double-check scope next time |

### Bounty Negotiation

If you believe a severity is incorrect:
1. **Present additional evidence** — "Here's how this can chain with..."
2. **Show business impact** — "Attackers could manipulate payments"
3. **Reference similar bounties** — From the same program
4. **Be humble** — "I understand triage's assessment, but wanted to share additional context"

Do NOT:
- Threaten to disclose publicly
- Demand specific amounts
- Get emotional or aggressive

## Building Reputation

| Action | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Clean, professional reports | Faster triage, better bounties |
| Responsive communication | Triagers remember you |
| Valid bugs, low duplicates | Invites to private programs |
| Helping other hunters | Networking, knowledge sharing |
| Writing public writeups | Establishes expertise |
