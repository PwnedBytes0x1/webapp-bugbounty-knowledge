# Ethical Hacking Basics

## What is Ethical Hacking?

Ethical hacking is the authorized practice of bypassing system security to identify potential data breaches and threats in a network.

## Types of Hackers

- **White Hat** - Ethical hackers with permission
- **Black Hat** - Malicious hackers without authorization
- **Gray Hat** - May violate laws but without malicious intent

## Core Principles

1. Authorization - Never test without written permission
2. Confidentiality - Protect discovered data
3. Integrity - Do not damage systems or data
4. Scope Compliance - Stay within agreed boundaries
5. Responsible Disclosure - Report findings professionally

## Ethical Hacking Phases

1. Reconnaissance - Gathering information
2. Scanning and Enumeration - Finding live hosts and services
3. Gaining Access - Exploiting vulnerabilities
4. Maintaining Access - Persistence (pentest context)
5. Covering Tracks - Cleaning up (only when authorized)
